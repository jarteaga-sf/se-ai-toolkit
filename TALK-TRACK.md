## SECTION 1: The Big Picture -- "What"

### Slide 1: The Opening Quote

> "Customers expect tailored demos, fast POCs, and solutions that fit their business. AI tools let any SE deliver that."

**Say something like:**

"Let's start with a simple truth. Our customers don't care how we build things. They care that what we show them feels like it was built for them. That's what these tools make possible -- and you don't need to be a developer to use them. You just need to know what your customer needs."

### Slide 2: Manual Everything vs. Directing Outcomes

**Say something like:**

"This is the shift. On the left is how most of us work today -- every demo tweak, every data change, every build, done manually. On the right is where we're headed. You describe what the customer needs, and the tool builds it. You're not writing code. You're directing outcomes."

**If someone asks 'But I don't code':**

"That's the whole point. These tools don't require you to be a developer. If you can describe what a customer needs in plain English -- 'show me a dashboard for a healthcare company with 500 cases a month' -- these tools can build it."

### Slide 3: What This Unlocks for SEs

**Say something like:**

"Three things unlock when you start using these tools."

- **Speed:** "You can deliver a tailored POC before the next call. Not next quarter -- before the next call."
- **Credibility:** "Imagine walking into a prospect's world with a demo that already looks like their business. That changes the conversation."
- **Independence:** "If you can describe what the customer needs, you can make it real. You don't need to file a ticket. You don't need to wait for anyone."

### Slide 4: Agentforce Context

**Say something like:**

"And here's why this matters right now. Agentforce is the biggest bet we have. But adoption doesn't happen because we sold a license -- it happens because customers see it working for their business. SEs who can stand up a working agent in a single session? That's how we drive activation. These tools make that possible for every one of us."

### Slide 5: Takeaway

**Say something like:**

"So the takeaway here is simple. You don't need to write code. You need to describe what your customer needs clearly enough that the tool can build it. That's the skill we're building today."

## SECTION 2: The Big Picture -- "How"

### Slide 1: Three Rockets, One Running Shoe

**Say something like:**

"Alright, so we're covering four tools today. Here's how I think about them. Three of these are rockets -- they give you 10x, sometimes 100x productivity on the right task. One is a running shoe -- it makes you 20-30% faster at something you already do. Both matter. But they're different, and knowing when to reach for which one is the skill."

### Slide 2: The Tool Cards

**Walk through each briefly:**

- **Saleo:** "This is the running shoe. No code, no setup headaches, just a Chrome extension. You swap in prospect-specific data on your Salesforce org without touching real records. If you do nothing else after this session, install Saleo. It's the lowest-effort, highest-frequency win."

- **MeshMesh:** "This is a rocket built specifically for Salesforce. It already understands the platform -- your data model, your rules, your patterns. One SE used it to cut demo guide prep from 2 full days down to 30 minutes. Another built a six-week POC in an hour and a half."

- **Cursor:** "This is a smart editor with AI built into every action. If you've used any code editor before, it'll feel familiar. Four keyboard shortcuts cover 90% of what you need. SEs are using it to cut custom demo prep from hours to minutes."

- **Claude Code:** "This is the most powerful and the most autonomous. You describe a goal, and it handles everything -- reads your project, writes code, runs it, checks its own work. One SE built a full Agentforce agent with 4 topics and 500+ tests in about an hour, just using natural language."

### Slide 3: The Engines Are Here

**Say something like:**

"The tools exist. They're available to us right now. The gap isn't access -- it's muscle memory. This session is about closing that gap."

### Slide 4: What Makes These Tools Click

**Say something like:**

"Four things to keep in mind as we go deeper."

- **Describe, Don't Build:** "You describe the goal, not the steps. The tool figures out how to get there."
- **Context is Everything:** "The more the tool knows about your project, the better the output. A short setup doc goes a long way. Generic AI gives you generic answers. AI with context gives you something you can actually use."
- **Your SE Knowledge Is the Edge:** "Your domain experience is your superpower here. The better you describe what the customer needs, the better the output. AI doesn't replace your knowledge -- it amplifies it."
- **Customer Impact:** "This isn't about us being more productive for the sake of it. The faster we build, the faster customers activate. Tailored demos, POCs, hands-on proof -- that's what drives retention."

### Slide 5: Takeaway

**Say something like:**

"All four tools are worth exploring. By the end of this session, you'll have a playbook to start using them today. And I mean today -- not next week, not after some training course. Today."

## HANDLING COMMON QUESTIONS

**"I'm not technical. Is this for me?"**
"Saleo requires zero technical skill. Cursor feels like a text editor with superpowers. Start there. You'll be surprised how far plain English gets you."

**"Is this approved/compliant?"**
"Saleo, Cursor, and Claude Code are available through the Software Catalog today. MeshMesh requires procurement approval. For emerging use cases like org analysis, we're working through compliance -- that's why they're labeled 'Emerging' on the site."

**"How is this different from ChatGPT?"**
"ChatGPT is a chat window. These tools are built into your workflow -- they read your project files, understand your Salesforce org, and deploy directly. The output isn't a conversation; it's working code."

**"What if it writes bad code?"**
"It will sometimes. Think of it as a fast first draft. Your job is to review, test, and refine. That's why we recommend demo orgs and always reviewing the changes before you approve."

**"Where do I start?"**
"Install Saleo today. It's 10 minutes and you'll use it on your next demo. After that, request a Cursor license and try building one component from a prompt. That's your first week."
