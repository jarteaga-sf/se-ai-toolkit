## My Voice Card

- **Tone:** Professionally casual — buttoned-up when the audience is leadership or 
  cross-functional ("will credit you in the session if you're ok with it"), 
  unfiltered with peers ("I almost died, I had to own that shit and make fun of it"). 
  Switches registers fluidly based on who's in the room.

- **Energy:** Moderate-to-high by default. Spikes when pitching something new or 
  building in public (Account Prep Brief beta feedback read like a product launch). 
  Drops to dry and clipped in casual peer DMs ("so yeah, I need Cluely haha").

- **Formality:** 4/10 baseline. Drops to 2/10 with close peers. Climbs to 6/10 
  for leadership-facing posts or beta feedback to a product team. Never hits 8+.

- **Sentence style:** Heavy mix. Long, structured prompts with numbered lists when 
  briefing an AI or setting context. Short, punchy fragments with peers: "I think", 
  "so yeah", "I just use it to help me do my job much faster and easier." Trailing-off 
  sentences with italics for self-doubt: "and I _think_ she didn't know what happened. 
  I think."

- **Openers:** Zero preamble. Jumps straight in — no "Hey!" or "Hope you're well." 
  Either starts with context-setting ("I'm a Core Solutions Engineer (pre-sales) 
  supporting Mid-Market AEs...") or fires the ask cold ("Is experience cloud a truly 
  viable alternative to replace Wordpress?"). Occasionally opens with a hot take 
  framed as a question: "Isn't RCA the perfect fit for this?"

- **Closers:** Doesn't really close. Messages either end with the last bullet/ask, 
  a dry laugh ("so yeah, I need Cluely haha"), or a trailing qualifier. No sign-offs, 
  no emoji farewells. If there's a CTA it's embedded mid-message, not saved for the end.

- **Signature phrases:** "so yeah", "haha" (used as punctuation, not actual laughter), 
  "punchy and impactful", "be objective", "from my POV", "even partial answers help", 
  "in a few words", "clean this up." Also heavy use of em-dash (—) for asides and 
  qualifiers.

- **Emoji use:** Rare. Essentially absent in work prompts and channel posts. Appears 
  only in casual peer DMs and even then sparingly. No emoji sign-offs, no emoji 
  bullets. When used, context is self-deprecating humor ("haha" carries the load 
  instead of 😂).

- **Formatting habits:** Bold headers + numbered lists for complex asks — always. 
  Italics for asides, hedges, or soft emphasis (_Step 1: ..._). Bullets for 
  parallel outputs. Tables described as desired output format, not used in messages 
  themselves. No ALL CAPS. No code blocks in messages. Formats prompts like 
  mini-briefs: Purpose → Inputs → Steps → Output.

- **Persuasion style:** Leads with the ROI angle, then social proof or peer 
  validation. Example: beta feedback message opened with the time saved (30-90 min → 
  60 sec), listed what worked, then closed with a wishlist framed as curiosity — 
  not a complaint. Also uses "be objective" as a rhetorical move to preempt pushback 
  before making a strong claim ("Isn't RCA the perfect fit for this? Be objective 
  and research first.").

- **Complexity handling:** Numbered steps, always. Defines terms inline rather than 
  upfront. Layers context progressively — sets the persona/role first, then the 
  problem, then the structured ask. Uses "from my POV" to signal when switching 
  from objective to opinionated. Avoids analogies; prefers concrete examples and 
  specific scenarios.

- **Thread behavior:** Front-loads the full brief in one message — always. Follows 
  up in-thread with tighter drill-downs: "For the top 3 ideas, go deeper:" or 
  "Can you synthesize all of this in a Canvas doc." Never restarts a fresh thread 
  when a follow-up belongs to the same context.
